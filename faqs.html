Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about the MSIS program

Typically, new MSIS students take foundational courses in their first semester. Consult with your academic advisor, Kathleen Barker, to create a personalized course plan based on your background and career goals. Common first-semester courses include programming fundamentals, database management, and business analytics.

Class registration is done through the UC Portal. After meeting with your advisor, you'll receive a registration PIN. Log into your student portal, navigate to "Student Services," select "Registration," and add your courses. Make sure to register during your designated time slot.

Visit our TA Documents page for detailed information. Contact Kathleen Barker to express your interest and learn about available positions. You'll need to complete I-9 verification, visit ISSO (if international), and obtain your SSN before starting.

MSIS students have access to the Career Development Center, Academic Success Center, Libraries, Computer Labs, Student Recreation Center, and various student organizations. The Lindner College also offers networking events, workshops, and career fairs throughout the year.

Start preparing from your first semester by building your resume, networking, and attending career events. Most students secure internships for their second summer. Work with the Career Development Center and connect with Kathleen Barker for guidance on finding opportunities aligned with your career goals.

Yes! UC Lindner offers several graduate certificate programs that can complement your MSIS degree, including Business Analytics, Cybersecurity, and Digital Marketing. Speak with Kris Jones or Kathleen Barker to learn more about certificate options and how they fit into your degree plan.

Popular neighborhoods for graduate students include Clifton, Corryville, and Oakley. On-campus housing options are also available. Check UC's Off-Campus Housing Office for listings, connect with current students through our Student Ambassadors, and join MSIS student groups for roommate searches and housing recommendations.

A car is not necessary. UC offers free shuttle services, and students get free Metro bus passes. The campus is walkable, and bike-sharing options are available. Check our Transportation page for more details. If you do bring a car, parking permits are available through UC Parking Services.
