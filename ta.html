Teaching Assistantship Resources

Everything you need to know about becoming a TA at UC Lindner

What is a Teaching Assistantship?

A Teaching Assistantship (TA) is a valuable opportunity for graduate students to gain teaching experience, develop professional skills, and receive financial support while pursuing their MSIS degree at UC Lindner.

As a TA, you'll work closely with faculty members, assist in course instruction, support student learning, and contribute to the academic community. This experience not only provides financial benefits but also enhances your resume and prepares you for future career opportunities in academia or industry.

How to Apply for TA

Interested in becoming a Teaching Assistant? Here's how to get started:

  • Meet the eligibility requirements (good academic standing)
  • Express interest to your professors or department
  • Submit your application through the department portal
  • Contact Kathleen Barker, Academic Advisor, for guidance and further instructions
Need Help? Reach out to Kathleen Barker (Academic Advisor) for personalized guidance on the TA application process.

Documents Required to Get Started

Before you can begin your TA position, you'll need to complete the following:

  1. Complete I-9 Verification
    Employment eligibility verification required by U.S. law
  2. Visit ISSO (International Student & Scholar Office)
    For international students: obtain work authorization documents
  3. Obtain Your Social Security Number (SSN)
    Required for payroll processing and tax purposes
Important: Start these processes early as they may take several weeks to complete.

TA Responsibilities

What you'll do as a Teaching Assistant:

  • Assist with course instruction and lab sessions
  • Grade assignments and provide feedback
  • Hold office hours to support students
  • Prepare course materials and presentations
  • Communicate with students via email and Canvas
  • Attend required training sessions

Benefits of Being a TA

Why become a Teaching Assistant?

  • Financial Support: Stipend and/or tuition reduction
  • Professional Development: Enhance teaching and communication skills
  • Networking: Build relationships with faculty and students
  • Resume Builder: Valuable experience for academic or industry careers
  • Academic Insight: Deepen your understanding of course material
