Build the perfect schedule for your MSIS journey

Plan Your Academic Path

Strategic course selection is key to maximizing your MSIS experience. Use these tips and tools to create a balanced, effective schedule.

1. Meet with Your Advisor First

Why it matters: Your advisor knows degree requirements, prerequisite chains, and can help you avoid scheduling conflicts.

Action: Schedule a meeting with Kathleen Barker before each registration period to review your plan.

2. Balance Core and Electives

Why it matters: Taking all core courses at once can be overwhelming. Mix in electives for variety.

Action: Aim for 2-3 core courses + 1-2 electives per semester for a balanced workload.

View Curriculum Requirements ‚Üí
‚è∞

3. Check Prerequisites

Why it matters: Some advanced courses require foundational classes. Missing prerequisites delays graduation.

Action: Review course descriptions on Catalyst for prerequisite requirements before enrolling.

üë•

4. Read Professor Reviews

Why it matters: Teaching styles vary. Find professors whose approach matches your learning style.

Action: Check RateMyProfessors and ask current students for recommendations.

üéØ

5. Align with Career Goals

Why it matters: Electives let you specialize in areas relevant to your target career path.

Action: Choose electives that build skills for your desired role (analytics, security, etc.).

Explore Career Paths ‚Üí
üìÖ

6. Consider Your Schedule

Why it matters: Back-to-back classes, early mornings, or late evenings may not work for everyone.

Action: Leave buffer time between classes for commuting, meals, and study breaks.

üíº

7. Plan for Work/Life Balance

Why it matters: Overloading can lead to burnout. Consider TA/GA work, internships, and personal commitments.

Action: Most students take 9-12 credit hours per semester (3-4 courses).

üîÑ

8. Have a Backup Plan

Why it matters: Popular courses fill quickly. Registration errors happen.

Action: Identify 1-2 alternative courses in case your first choice is full.

‚ùì Common Questions

Q: How many classes should I take per semester?

A: Most students take 3-4 courses (9-12 credits). Full-time is 9+ credits, part-time is less than 9.

Q: Can I change my schedule after registering?

A: Yes! There's an add/drop period at the start of each semester. Check the academic calendar for exact dates.

Q: What if a required course is full?

A: Join the waitlist and contact your advisor. They may be able to secure a seat or suggest alternatives.

Q: When is my registration time slot?

A: Check your registration time on Catalyst. It's based on credit hours completed‚Äîmore credits = earlier registration.
